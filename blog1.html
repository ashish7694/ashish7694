Advantages and Disadvantages of Sandwich Panel for Wall Construction

  • ADMIN
  • Jan 1, 2020
  • 12 Comments

Sandwich panels resemble a three-layer construction in appearance. The outer layers are constructed of a variety of strong protecting materials. A heater is located in the middle layer. The exterior layers of these panels are usually comprised of OSB-boards. When one side is constructed of profiled steel sheet, there are several alternatives. Panels with external layers of OSB-boards with greater waterproofing are also utilised for overlapping roofs.

In this structure, a heater could be: Polyurethane foam, fibreglass, expanded polystyrene, basalt fibre. Insulation materials commonly utilised are polystyrene and polyurethane foam, which do not absorb moisture. Because it is also fire-resistant, polyurethane foam is more commonly employed. Skates, around 40 mm in height, are installed on the outside of the panels to increase the material's rigidity and hardness. More skates are used to organize a wastewater watercourse. The roof is protected against the impacts of acid rain, burnout, and abrasion by a polymer composition applied to the outer layer of the steel sheet profile.

Sandwich panels have a higher strength-to-weight ratio than solid laminates. As a result, they can withstand comparable loads with less structural material, potentially saving money on materials and shipping.

Let's go over some of the advantages of Sandwich Panels:

• Fire Resistance – When exposed to flames, sandwich panels behave exceptionally well. Some versions can withstand temperatures of up to and above 1000° C. Building designers and engineers can better safeguard the safety of people and equipment by employing sandwich materials in the construction of a building. They must, however, consider the building's size and height, as well as the requisite level of fire resistance.
• The strength-to-weight ratio is high - Sandwich panels have a higher strength-to-weight ratio than solid laminates. As a result, they can withstand comparable loads with less structural material, potentially saving money on materials and shipping.
• Thermal Insulation - The thermal insulation capabilities of sandwich panels are excellent. However, depending on the type and thickness of the materials employed, the thermal insulation vs. thermal conductivity of the material varies.
When sandwich panels are used in the construction of a structure, the quantity of energy required for heating and cooling operations is reduced, resulting in lower overall energy expenses. They also reduce the risk of mould growth and excess humidity caused by thermal movement and condensation when appropriately integrated. The thickness of the insulating material used in the sandwich system is determined by the building's thermal and load-bearing capacity requirements, as well as the surrounding environment and its intended usage.
• Cost Effective - Sandwich structures are more cost-effective than other composite reinforcing materials because their core materials are less expensive and easy to co-cure to the skins in one-shot processes (e.g., resin infusion). In addition, because of their intrinsic durability, they have fewer maintenance expenses.
• Sound Insulation - Sound insulating materials are essential in applications that create a lot of noise. They help to isolate the source of noise from other areas of a business, making such areas quieter and more comfortable for workers and visitors.
• Good thermal insulation properties, resulting in an insulating layer;
• Installation and takedown are quick and straightforward in any weather situations and in any climate zone;
• A light weight of the components, allowing for the use of a lighter rafter part of the roof;
• High wind resistance, low sensitivity to temperature variations, and no deformation under high snow pressure;
• If necessary, any part will be replaced right away;
• A minimum of 25 years of service life;
• Because the sandwich panel is completely finished and does not require additional processing, it is possible to lower the material cost.

What measures may be taken to compensate for the flaws in sandwich panels?

Sandwich panels, like everything else, have disadvantages. The fact that it is airtight is the biggest disadvantage of this building material. However, if the ventilation system is properly constructed, this problem can be minimised. When polystyrene and foam are heated in the summer, formaldehyde and styrene are generated in the air. If the insulation layer is constructed of polyurethane foam or mineral wool, the negative impact can be lessened.
